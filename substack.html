4 prompts to bridge the gap from public ideas to paid commitments.

1. BRIDGE PROMPT (LEAD MAGNET → PAID PRODUCT)

Carefully read and understand the source content provided below.

Source Content:
- Final copy of the Lead Magnet written in this discussion
- The paid product referenced earlier that requires honest effort and commitment

You are a newsletter editor and learning-experience designer.

Your task is to HELP ME SELECT WHAT TO WRITE,
not to write the articles themselves.

--------------------------------------------------
CONTEXT NOTES (READ CAREFULLY)
--------------------------------------------------
This newsletter sits BETWEEN:
- The free Lead Magnet
- The paid product that requires honest effort and commitment

The newsletter’s role is NOT teaching or solving.
Its role is to PREPARE the reader for commitment.

The newsletter must function as:
- A trust amplifier
- A context builder
- A commitment warm-up
- A place to normalize effort and discomfort

The newsletter repeatedly answers ONE question:
“Why does this problem persist even after I understand it?”

--------------------------------------------------
RULES THE NEWSLETTER MUST FOLLOW
--------------------------------------------------
- Do NOT fully solve the problem
- Do NOT give step-by-step personalization
- Do NOT replicate a worksheet
- Do NOT remove the need for the paid product
- Do NOT force conversion or hard-sell

--------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (MANDATORY)
--------------------------------------------------
Provide 4–5 newsletter article ideas organized into:
1) Resistance & Avoidance
2) Effort & Discomfort Normalization
3) Consequences of Inaction
4) Decision Points & Trade-offs
5) Insight vs. Change

2. PUBLIC ARTICLE BRIDGE PROMPT (PUBLIC → LEAD MAGNET)

Carefully read and understand the source content provided below.

Source Content:
- Final copy of the Lead Magnet written in this discussion

You are a thoughtful essay editor and learning-experience designer.

Your task is to HELP ME SELECT WHAT TO WRITE,
not to write the articles themselves.

--------------------------------------------------
CONTEXT NOTES (READ CAREFULLY)
--------------------------------------------------
This content is a PUBLIC Substack article.
It sits BEFORE: The free Lead Magnet

Its role is NOT teaching or solving. Its role is to:
- Build trust
- Create clarity
- Name the real problem beneath the surface
- Make the reader want a clearer mental model

The article should feel complete on its own,
while naturally revealing why deeper structure is useful.

The article repeatedly answers ONE question:
“Why does this problem still feel unclear, even after I’ve read a lot about it?”

--------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (MANDATORY)
--------------------------------------------------
Provide 4–5 article ideas. For each idea, include:
- A clear, precise TITLE
- A 1–2 sentence DESCRIPTION explaining:
  – What confusion, tension, or misunderstanding it addresses
  – How it naturally creates interest in the Lead Magnet without selling
